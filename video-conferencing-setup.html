Video Conferencing Setup

In March 2020, I started working from home, spending between 20-40 hours per week on video calls. I find the 720p webcam that's built into Macbook Pros (even the 16-inch model) to be garbage, and considering I take nearly all of my calls from a desk, I decided it's reasonable to compromise a bit on mobility to upgrade the quality of my A/V setup.

Here are the results of my new setup compared to the default FaceTime HD Camera:

To get this to work, you can't just buy the camera; several items are needed for a working, high-quality, plug-and-play setup:

A few notes: the ADAI Z Flex Tilt Head works fine, but here's a higher quality alternative: edelkrone FlexTILT Head 2

You can get much cheapter HDMI capture cards. I tried this one—BlueAVS HDMI Capture Card ($22.99)—but the quality and video smoothness were noticeably bad, so I decided to pay extra for the elgato cam link.
