Video Conferencing Setup

In March 2020, I started working from home, spending between 20-40 hours per week on video calls. I find the 720p webcam that's built into Macbook Pros (even the 16-inch model) to be garbage, and considering I take nearly all of my calls from a desk, I decided it's reasonable to compromise a bit on mobility to upgrade the quality of my A/V setup.

I started with the Logitech BRIO 4K webcam, which boasted superior video quality with an easy setup; just one USB-C cable plugged directly into my laptop.

These are screen captures from a video session in Cisco Webex, the service I most used at work at the time. Because my needs are for video conferencing, I don't care about the raw video output from the camrea; I care what the image looks like after it's been compressed by video conferencing software, most of which today downscale resolution to 720p. As such, the benefit of recording in 4K are lost, and otherwise the video quality is marginally better: slightly sharper, with improved colors, and less noise (especially in low light), but not by much, and no noticeable background blurring.

I wasn't satisfied with these results.
